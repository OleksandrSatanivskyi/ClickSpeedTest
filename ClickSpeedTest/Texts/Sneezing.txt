Yes, you can sneeze with your eyes open. And, no, the schoolyard legend, "If you sneeze with your eyes open, your eyeballs will pop out of your head," isn’t true. Keep reading to learn more about the mechanisms of sneezing - and why our eyes automatically close when we do. Sneezing includes an autonomic reflex that closes your eyes. An autonomic reflex is a motor action that your body makes in response to a stimulus. It doesn’t involve a conscious decision on your part to take that action. It’s possible to sneeze with your eyes open, but most people have to make a concerted effort to override their reflex to keep their eyes open.
